I'm a 6th year PhD student in theoretical particle physics at Cornell University's Laboratory for Elementary Particle Physics (LEPP). With my advisor, Maxim Perelstein, I study collider phenomenology with a focus on developing new tools and techniques in the search for new physics beyond the Standard Model.

[Research page]   [GitHub page]   [INSPIRE record]   [arXiv record]



Born and raised in Chicago, I first encountered particle physics by reading Scientific American articles during my years at Maine South High School. Before coming to Cornell University for my PhD, I went to Michigan State University for undergrad degrees in physics and mathematics. Go State!
