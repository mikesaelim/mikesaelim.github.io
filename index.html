I'm a senior software engineer at OpenField, based out of Chicago. Before that, I've been a software engineer at Spotify, Civis Analytics, and GrubHub. I enjoy winter and naps.

In my previous career, I was a PhD student in theoretical particle physics at Cornell University's Laboratory for Elementary Particle Physics (LEPP). With my advisor, Maxim Perelstein, I researched collider phenomenology with a focus on developing new tools and techniques in the search for new physics beyond the Standard Model. Before Cornell, I went to Michigan State University for undergrad degrees in physics and mathematics. GO GREEN!
